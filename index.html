My name is Alanis. I am Mexican American and I can speak Spanish and English. Ever since I can remember, I was interested in helping my community. In highschool I was involved in countless clubs and organizations that allowed me to do exactly that, volunteer. I always did it for fun and I soon realized that I needed it for college and there was always something new to learn in every club I was in. There are so many skills that I learned in these clubs, and one of them was patience. In Key Club, we volunteered with edlers every Wednesday and I had to listen to them and be able to have a conversation. Sometimes they would talk very slow or for a long time and I learned to be patient with them.

I grew up in a small household with one brother and two loving parents. I chose to be an education major because when I want to work with kids and become a teacher. I am a sophmore at the University of California, Riverside. I think I am committed to improving who I am and what I stand for. Education is very important to me because of the impact a teacher has on a child. It is important the teacher is passionate so they are able to motivate their students to learn.

I am currently the treasurer for a club on Campus. The club is called Ballet Folklorico and we focus on Mexican Traditional Dances and its culture. I think being treasurer in college has challenged me. I have worked in panels, I have organized fundrasing events, filled out multiple documents and I have created budgets and keeping track of all the monetaries that are involved with the club. This was nothing I have done before and I have learned to organize meetings and have better communication skills.
